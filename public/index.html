  Wrap up

As a client side developer there have been scant opportunities for me to pursue my passion and use lisp in earnest. ClojureScript has unequivocally removed that obstacle, bringing a modern and powerful lisp to javascript environments.

At a glance, Clojure, a predominately functional language might not seem like a natural fit for client side development. Much of the discussion in the community seem to focus on mutable models, and finding ways to bind views onto those bits of mutable state. Can Clojure excel in such muddy waters?

Not so long ago I might have thought not. Then I discovered d3. Mike Bostocks d3 is a javascript library for manipulating documents, but what makes it unique is its focus on transformation not representation which all sounds very functional!
